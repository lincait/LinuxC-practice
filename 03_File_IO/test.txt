To address the limited accuracy of micro-Doppler feature
extraction in UAV rotor target detection, this paper investigates 
and validates an advanced extraction algorithm. Initially, the 
micro-Doppler effect of UAV rotors is analyzed through signal 
modeling, and the theoretical foundations of the STFT and FSST 
are systematically elaborated. Focusing on the challenges of 
path-crossing errors and zero-frequency interference prevalent 
in traditional ridge extraction algorithms when processing multi-component 
signals, this study proposes an improved algorithm based on a hybrid cost function and
a dwell penalty mechanism.
